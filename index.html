Joey Jordison

The best drummer in the history of metal and rock!

Here is a timeline of Joey Jordison's life:

  • 1975 - Joey Jordison was born in Des Moines, Iowa.
  • 1990 - Joey co-founded a band named Modifidious.
  • 1994 - Joey found a job at a Sinclair garage in Urbandale.
  • 1995 - Joey joined another band called The Rejects as a guitarist due to his previous band Modifidious, disbanding.
  • 1995 - In late 1995, Joey joined the band Slipknot.
  • 1996 - The first album of Slipknot was released, "Mate. Feed. Kill. Repeat."
  • 1999 - Slipknot then released their debut self-titled studio album "Slipknot".
  • 2001 - "Iowa" was released, which is the second studio album of Slipknot. This album was released under a lot of pressure due to the success of their 1999 self-debuted album Slipknot. During this period, the band members' relationships with each other suffered and it was described as the darkest and most tumultuous time of Slipknot's career.
  • 2008 - Joey broke his ankle and some English tour dates had to be canceled because of this.
  • 2009 - Joey then was taken to the A&E because of a burst appendix less than an hour before he was supposed to perform for Auburn, Washington's KISW Pain in the Grass concert and Slipknot had to cancel more shows so Joey could recover.
  • 2013 - Slipknot, on their official website, announced that Joey had left the band for personal reasons. Joey, in response to that, released an official statement insisting that he had actually been fired from the band and he claimed that "Slipknot has been my life for the last 18 years and I would never abandon it or my fans."
  • 2016 - After both sides were silent for 3 years, Joey revealed that he left the band because he suffered from transverse myelitis, a neurological disease that inhibited him from playing the drums at the end of his time with Slipknot.
  • 2021 - At the age of 46, Joey died in his sleep, the cause of his death is yet to be announced.

"Without blood and pain, there is no Slipknot."

-Joey Jordison
